01

Definitions: TAM, SAM and SOM

TAM (Total Addressable Market): the complete global universe of SMBs — formal and informal — and the GTV/TPV they generate. No geographic or size filters applied.

SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): SMBs with annual GTV up to the configured cap (default $1M). Includes all informal SMBs (100% sub-$1M by definition) and formal micro/small businesses within the threshold. Represents the segment BOSS can address with its current value proposition.

SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): subset of the SAM in countries where Loyverse is already active (internal list from "Where is the payment volume available?", Dec 2025). Immediate opportunity without geographic expansion.

Loyverse Revenue: SOM × configured penetration rate. Penetration represents the share of the SOM revenue potential captured by Loyverse.

Two scenarios: Conservative uses only the ~360M formally registered SMBs. Expanded adds informal businesses up to the 445M total cap (IFC MSME Finance Gap 2024), with a scaling factor of 0.1242 applied to raw country-level estimates.

02

Calculation Formulas

// Country-level inputs formal_micro_M, formal_small_M, formal_medium_M // millions of SMBs informal_raw_M // raw country estimate digital_pct // % of GTV transacted electronically // Expanded scenario INF_SCALE = 0.1242 // (445M - 360.3M formal) / 681.7M raw informal informal_M = informal_raw_M x INF_SCALE // expanded scenario informal_M = 0 // conservative scenario // TAM (USD billions: M SMBs x K USD/SMB = B USD) TAM_GTV = micro x gp.micro + small x gp.small + medium x gp.medium + inf x gp.informal TAM_TPV = TAM_GTV x digital_pct TAM_Rev = TAM_GTV x gtvRate + TAM_TPV x tpvRate // SAM -- SMBs with GTV <= cap // Qualification by income tier (% of each size class within threshold): // HI: micro=100%, small=60%, medium=5% // UMI: micro=100%, small=75%, medium=10% // LMI: micro=100%, small=95%, medium=30% // LI: micro=100%, small=99%, medium=65% // Informal: 100% (all sub-$1M by definition) SAM_GTV = micro x q_m x avg_m + small x q_s x avg_s + medium x q_d x avg_d + inf x gp.informal SAM_Rev = SAM_GTV x gtvRate + (SAM_GTV x digital_pct) x tpvRate // SOM = SAM restricted to countries where loyverse = true // Loyverse Revenue = SOM_Rev x penetration% // Total Revenue = GTV fee (platform) + TPV fee (payments processing)

GTV x gtvRate: platform fee (BOSS, all POS transactions recorded). TPV x tpvRate: payments processing revenue (Loyverse Payments). Estimated uncertainty: +/-20-25% developed markets, +/-40-50% emerging markets.

Income Tiers & Regional Definitions
TermDefinition
HIHigh Income (World Bank classification, GNI per capita >$13,845 in 2024). Loyverse HI countries: USA, UK, DE, FR, JP, AU, SG, HK, NZ, CH, SE, DK, FI, AT, NL, BE, IE, IL, SA, AE, OM, PR. Avg GTV per micro SMB: $80K/year. Highest TPV take rate feasibility.
UMIUpper Middle Income (GNI per capita $4,516-$13,845). Loyverse UMI: BR, MX, MY, TH, TR, CO, CL, AR, CR, PA, DZ, LB, KZ, PL. Avg GTV per micro: $30K/year. Strong Loyverse base; medium PSP complexity.
LMILower Middle Income (GNI per capita $1,146-$4,515). Loyverse LMI: PH, VN, EG, NG, KE, GH, ID (borderline), MA, JO, MN, UZ. Avg GTV per micro: $7K/year. High merchant count, lower unit revenue. Mobile money critical.
LILow Income (GNI per capita <$1,146). Loyverse LI: MM (Myanmar), KH (Cambodia), TZ (Tanzania). Avg GTV per micro: $3K/year. Mostly offline usage; Payments attach very low. Long-term potential as digitization increases.
GCCGulf Cooperation Council - Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, UAE. United by common currency peg (USD), Arab language, shared economic policy. Foodics and Mada are GCC-specific. Key Loyverse markets: SA ($1.27B), UAE ($251M), OM ($93M).
MENAMiddle East and North Africa - broader region including GCC + Egypt, Turkey, Morocco, Algeria, Jordan, Lebanon, Israel, Iraq, Iran. Loyverse SOM MENA TPV: $1.88B. Most diverse region in Loyverse's portfolio by language, regulation, and digital maturity.
SEASoutheast Asia - Indonesia, Thailand, Philippines, Malaysia, Vietnam, Singapore, Myanmar, Cambodia (and Brunei, Laos, Timor-Leste). Loyverse's highest-TPV region cluster ($7.4B). Characterized by high informal economy share and local payment rail fragmentation.
SSASub-Saharan Africa - all African countries south of the Sahara. Loyverse SOM countries: NG, ZA, KE, GH, TZ, MU, SC. Mobile money dominant. Very high local share. Lowest ARPM potential in short term; highest long-term growth rate.
LATAMLatin America - in this document: BR, MX (counted in North America for TPV), CO, CL, AR, CR, PA. Note: Mexico ($3B TPV) is treated separately as North America in Loyverse's internal TPV data. LATAM (excl. MX) Loyverse TPV: ~$0.8B.
APACAsia-Pacific - in this document covers all Asian and Pacific markets including SEA, EA (JP, KR, TW, HK), SG, AU, NZ. Loyverse APAC TPV: $7.4B. The largest single Loyverse TPV region.
Sources, Organizations & Benchmarks
TermDefinition
IFCInternational Finance Corporation (World Bank Group). Source for global MSME count (445M total including informal). Also publishes the MSME Finance Gap report ($5.7T financing gap for formal MSMEs).
World Bank MSME EIWorld Bank MSME Economic Indicators Database. Provides formal MSME counts by country. 196M micro-enterprises + 11M small enterprises globally (formal registered). Used as basis for the Conservative TAM scenario.
Worldpay GPRWorldpay Global Payments Report - published annually, covers 40 markets. Source for digital payment penetration % by country and payment method splits (card/wallet/A2A). 2025 edition used for TPV estimates.
ADB Asia SME MonitorAsian Development Bank's annual SME data publication for 24-26 Asia-Pacific economies. Source for APAC SME counts (Indonesia 65.5M, Thailand 3.2M, Philippines 1.1M, etc.). Most authoritative APAC SME data source.
EC SME FactsheetEuropean Commission SME Performance Review, published annually. Source for EU-27 SME count (26.1M) and country-level breakdowns. Includes Austria, Belgium, Switzerland, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, Greece, etc.
SEBRAEServiço Brasileiro de Apoio às Micro e Pequenas Empresas - Brazil's SME support agency. Source for Brazilian SME count (19M micro and small businesses). The most authoritative BR SME data.
US SBAUS Small Business Administration. Published 36.2M small businesses in US (2026 data). Definition: <500 employees. Source for US SME count and small business statistics.
CAGRCompound Annual Growth Rate - annualized growth rate over a multi-year period. Global POS software market CAGR: ~14-15% through 2030. Loyverse APAC TPV growing at ~15-25% CAGR. Used to project market size forward.
ARR / MRRAnnual/Monthly Recurring Revenue - the subscription revenue base, excluding one-time payments. Lightspeed 97% recurring. Toast ~20% SaaS. Loyverse Phase 1: 100% ARR (add-on subscriptions). Phase 2 adds transaction-based revenue (not recurring by definition, but highly predictable).
EBITDAEarnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation and Amortization - proxy for operating profitability. SumUp EBITDA-positive since Q4 2022. Toast 35% adjusted EBITDA margins (Q3 2025). Loyverse Phase 2 profitability target not publicly stated.
IDCInternational Data Corporation - technology market research firm. Source cited for Linx's 40.2% market share in Brazilian retail management software.
Mordor IntelligenceMarket research firm. Source for global POS software market size (~$12B 2024) and SME segment share (61% of total POS market). Also source for CAGR projections and regional market shares.
GSMAGlobal System for Mobile Communications Association - mobile industry trade body. Source for mobile money data in SSA (M-Pesa, MTN MoMo, Airtel Money subscriber counts and transaction volumes).
McKinsey Digital Payments SurveyAnnual McKinsey survey on US and European consumer digital payment behavior. Source for US wallet penetration (43% in-store in 2024, up from 23% in 2019) and European payment method splits.
